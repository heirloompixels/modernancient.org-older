Modern Ancient.

The summer sun followed by the summer rains. Seeds and weeds, oh so many weeds. Broken looms and worn out brushes. Rusty cans and cobblestone walkways. Elephants crushing mud houses because rage (senseless murder).

Our work: dressed up passion and the fighting words of the post modern robot. A direct repulsion for the mass produced yet embraced revolutionary distribution networks. Borrow heavily when necessary from the modern world but live in the ancient.

There are stories and then there are compulsions. An honesty derived from mud stained finger nails and a devotion to feelings the seasons of our great earth. Nomads at heart we borrow from the homesteader simply to feed ourselves poison free.

We are all slowly going the way of the buffalo but before them we skip sunsets off of water falls in the empty corner wilderness between electrons and commuter nightmares. The ancestral lineage pulses deep in our ground paws we can't help but sing to the moon and instagram it later. This is modern ancient.

Jeannie Ortiz

Weaver and body decorator. Hanger froms silks. Ariel daredevil.

Kyle Cunningham

Jeannie Ortiz & Kyle Cunningham

Modern Ancient is a way of life we've been experimenting with for the last decade and change together. At it's core is a diachotomy of ideals - using the best of what there is on offer now with the best of the wisdom from the old world as well. We strive to realize this in all our ideals from our art to the way we grow our own food.
